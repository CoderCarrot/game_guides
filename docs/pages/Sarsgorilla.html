Requirements (Trouble in Paradise)

Appear Requirements

  • There is a Bonboon resident in the garden.

Visit Requirements

  • You have 20 square pinometers of long grass (20%).
  • Have 3 Monkeynut trees in the garden.
  • Have a baby Bonboon in the garden.

Resident Requirements

  • Has eaten 4 Coconut Macaroons.
  • Has eaten 6 Bananas.
  • Has eaten 4 jars of Peanut Butter.

Romance Requirements

  • Is wearing a Funky Tie.
  • Has eaten 2 pieces of Bread.
  • Has eaten 1 jar of Marmalade.
  • Have a Sarsgorilla house in the garden.

Trick Requirements

  • Feed it a Monkeynut to have it perform trick 1.
  • Feed it a Banana Split to have it perform trick 2.

Variant 1 (Red)

  • Has eaten an Apple.

Variant 2 (Green)

  • Has eaten a bowl of Pea Soup.

Variant 3 (White)

  • Has eaten a Bone.

Wildcard Variants

  • Tail
  • Horns
  • Hairy back

Frequently Asked Questions

I've spent days and days trying to get a Sarsgorilla to be a resident with no luck. Is this a glitch?

Like the Elephanilla (see answer) and the Chippopotamus (see answer), this is another pinata that takes time to reside. As with any pinata that needs multiple visits, it's important to keep in mind that you probably won't get exactly the same Sarsgorilla, the next time a wild one visits your garden. Always make sure to maintain 3 Monkeynut trees and 1 baby Bonboon in the garden. (One method to having a baby Bonboon in the garden is to tap it's a cocoon immediately with the trick stick. The pinata is the same size and treated as a baby, but will not cocoon again.) If its Visit requirements are no longer met, it will leave the garden early, drawing out the number of visits needed. If you've got a visitor that only needs to meet one or two more requirements, try not to distract it with other items that it no longer is required to eat. One tip is to buy and tinker the fruits (Coconuts and Monkeynuts) and Bananas that a particular visitor needs to eat, and move the produce or bananas near where he visits/eats, to help speed things along. However, the Sarsgorilla needs to eat different things to reside, from the visit requirements (except for the monkeynuts tinkered from the monkeynuts from the monkeynut tree), unlike the Elephanilla, the Bonboon, the Galagoogoo, the Chewnicorn, the Roario, and the Chippopotamus, but like the Eaglair (see answer) and the Limeoceros (see answer). This way, it will hopefully eat more of what it needs to eat, instead of randomly devouring whatever it's fond of. Ultimately, patience is the key, while you pay attention to its needs, and keep your garden well-planted. (Either that, or you could pay someone off to ship you one.)
