When you see the Crowla, think less "dark, sinister, grave robber" and think more "garbage collector." It may stop that shiver tickling your spine. Introduce it to Dastardos, and give yourself a break.

For the sour variant of the Crowla, see Sour Crowla. For a list of all sour species, see the Sour Species category page.

The Crowla is a dark purple crow piñata. Its beak is a lighter shade of purple than the rest of its body and a different material, which is mostly made of dark purple, and the upper part of the beak has ridges at the bottom. It has red eyelids, and a long tuft of feathers. It has small wings, and its small feet are yellow.

Requirements (Original)

Appear Requirements

  • Have a resident Crowla in the garden.

Visit Requirements

  • Four Lickatoads in the garden.

Resident Requirements

  • Has eaten 2 Lickatoads.

Romance Requirements

  • Has eaten 2 Shellybeans.
  • Have a Crowla house in the garden

Variant 1 Requirements (Pink)

  • Has eating a Water Lily changes its color to pink.

Variant 2 Requirements (Orange)

  • Feeding it a Bird of Paradise flower changes its color to orange

Variant 3 Requirements (Blue)

  • Feeding it a Bluebell Seed changes its color to blue

Crowla Uses

  • Having 1 Crowla in the garden helps to meet the Crowla's Appear requirements.
  • Eating 1 Crowla helps to meet the Vulchurro's Resident requirements.
  • Directing a tamed Crowla to Dastardos will momentarily distract him, earning the player an achievement.

Commonly Asked Questions

My Crowlas get depressed really quickly and leave the garden? How can I keep them happy?

While a temporary fix is to feed them Happy candy, the real issue has to do with whether they like your garden or not. Like any other species, Crowlas like to have their Appear and Visit requirements met to remain happy in a garden. Once you have four Lickatoads in your garden, they should stop getting sad and leaving.

In general, once you've tamed a sour, ask yourself, "Does my garden have what it needs to attract a tame one from the wild?" If not, the one you've just tamed won't stay long, because the garden's not attractive to them.
