The Camello is known as the 'ship of the desert'. This is not because the Camello glides gracefully over the sand like a yacht slicing through the ocean waves. Oh no, it's because if you ride the Camello, you end up feeling sick.

The Camello is a purple dromedary piñata introduced in Viva Piñata: Trouble in Paradise. It can only be found in the Dessert Desert.

Mind those jaws! And the spit! And hooves! You can see why that barbarian fellow ended up socking one of these in the face. Plainly some folks around here have more affection for the, er, noble desert steed.

It has very long legs and its mouth is orange.

Requirements (Trouble in Paradise)

This piñata can only be found in the Dessert Desert.

Appear Requirements

  • You are a level 15 gardener or better.

Bait Requirements

  • A Camello enjoys orange-colored stuff (Yam).
Note: Bait the trap with a yam.

Resident Requirements

  • Have 20 square pinometers of sand (20%).
  • Has drunk 2 cups of coffee.
  • Has eaten 3 cacti.

Romance Requirements

  • Have 25 square pinometers of sand (25%).
  • Has eaten 3 coconut macaroons.
  • Is wearing Cool Shades.
  • Is wearing Señor Sombrero.
  • There is a Camello house in the garden.
1/3

Variant 1 Requirements (Pink)

  • Feeding it a water lily seed will change its color to pink.

Variant 2 Requirements (Orange)

  • Feeding it a jack o' lantern will change its color to orange.

Variant 3 Requirements (Blue)

  • Feeding it a bluebell seed will change its color to blue.
1/2

Wildcard Variants

  • Large hump on chin
  • Taller hump
  • Large ears

Trick Requirements

  • Has eaten a Palm Tree seed.
  • Has eaten a Cactus seed.

Requirements (Trouble in Paradise - Just for Fun)

  • Attract it by becoming a level 0 gardener or better.

Romance dance music

They love to groove with funk.

Camello Uses

  • Eating 1 Camello help meets the Tigermisu Romance requirements.

Species Conflicts

Scared of

  • Roario (Avoids or escapes garden if resident)

Rivals

Happiness Tips

  • Camellos like eating cacti, yams, monkeynuts, palm seeds, and sunflowers.
  • Camellos dislike eating all produce.
  • Camellos hate eating fireweeds.
