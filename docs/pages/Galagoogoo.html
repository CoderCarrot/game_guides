Galagoogoo

Did the Galagoogoo sit in thorn bushes once too often? Even now, scientists are coming to blows to decide if this animal is nocturnal because it has huge eyes or if it grew huge eyes because it couldn't get up in the day.

The Galagoogoo is a reclusive pink bush baby piñata that can only be encountered at night. They have an attraction for Tulips, Moon-on-a-Sticks and has Mothdrops as their evening snack.

The Galagoogoo make cry-like sounds while they’re awake, and even when sleeping. They love to climb trees and seem to fascinate the Chewnicorns and Orchids. They are also nocturnal, and only visit the garden during the night. Galagoogoos also love peace and quiet, and don’t like to be in immediate danger, which is why they prefer gardens with full and complete Tower of Sours.

Appearance

The Galagoogoo is a monkey-like piñata covered mostly in pink. It has a tan chest, a tan tail end, and partially tan ears. It has big, bright eyes with light blue irises, along with a small nose and lips. They also love to make cry noises and can usually be seen climbing trees or hopping around your garden. During the day, it is most likely they are sleeping. Don't confuse it with a kitten despite its looks.

Requirements (Original)

Appear Requirements

  • Have a completed Tower of Sour (8 pieces total).

Visit Requirements

  • Have a Moon-on-a-Stick in the garden.
  • Have 8 Mothdrops or 16 tulips in the garden.

Resident Requirements

  • Has eaten 4 tulips.
  • Has eaten 2 Mothdrops.
  • You have a groundskeeper in the garden.

Romance Requirements

  • Has eaten 4 orchids.
  • Have a Chewnicorn resident in the garden.
  • Have a Galagoogoo house in the garden.

Variant 1 Requirements (Yellow)

  • Feeding it a Sunflower changes its color to yellow.

Variant 2 Requirements (Purple)

  • Feeding it a loaf of Bread changes its color to purple.

Variant 3 Requirements (Green)

  • Feeding it Poison Ivy changes its color to green.

Note: Each of the three color variants has a base value of only 100 coins.

Rumors

  • Leafos states that Galagoogoos love Sunglasses because of their big eyes. True. Galagoogoos are more likely to stay awake during the day if they’re wearing Sunglasses.

Other Information

Galagoogoos dislike being watered by the watering can

Galagoogoos can climb trees. To get them down, tap the tree.

Galagoogoos sleep with one eye open when at full Candiosity.

The Galagoogoo won't become a resident until you have a helper, such as a Sprinkling, and they won't even visit until you have a Moon-on-a-Stick. So get a helper, prepare your Mothdrops and Tulips, and THEN put a Moon-on-a-Stick in the garden. Galagoogoos like tulip flowers and orchids, but any other flower will lower their Candiosity. You must not feed them milk, honey, or any other Costolot foods, such as bread, unless you wish to unlock the purple variant.

Glitches

  • Variant Galagoogoos only have a base value of 100 Chocolate Coins in the original Viva Piñata. Although this isn’t good news for your wallet, it does prevent it from being a highly likely target for Professor Pester.

In the TV Series

Les Galagoogoo

Les Galagoogoo is one of the main characters of the Viva Piñata TV series. He is the smartest piñata, but never utters a word. He only talks in strange hoots and sighs so no one ever understands him, but they somehow figure it out by themselves directly after he says it, and then claim that they found it which he dislikes.


Commonly Asked Questions

When I went to sell my Galagoogoo, it's only worth 100 coins. Anyone got any ideas why?

Some variants (Galagoogoos included) are worth less than their non-variant versions.

I've been trying to get a Galagoogoo to become a resident, but it won't stick around.

Because it's a high-level piñata, it might take an extra visit or two to get one to have eaten everything it needs, to reside. Also, it's important to keep in mind that you won't necessarily get exactly the same Galagoogoo visiting, the next time one shows up.

First, make sure you have far more than 16 Tulips or 8 Mothdrops in your garden, so it doesn't leave early after eating one or two.

Next, there's one thing you can do to help it along. Once it starts eating something, you can knock off some tulip flowerheads, or break open a Mothdrop, and leave the flowerheads/Life Candy near where it's eating. This will help save time, as the Galagoogoo only lingers until dusk. If you do it quickly enough, the Galagoogoo will gobble up what you're leaving for it, instead of wandering around. Make sure to keep the Watchling away from the Galagoogoo or it will be scared away.
