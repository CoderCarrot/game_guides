–Leafos after the Mousemallow appears.

The Mousemallow is a pink mouse piñata. It is cute, and a relatively happy piñata whose favorite catchphrase is “chu-chu!”

You can often see this piñata hiding in holes, eating Turnips and Yams. They are not very sociable, mainly because of how much it’s afraid of being squished, but thankfully it doesn’t have to worry about the Elephanilla - the only piñata that refuse to be around the pink creature.

Much is made of the Mousemallow’s hearing and the edge it provides against predators. Then again, it has no escape plan beyond 'run' - and for Syrupent and Kittyfloss families to thrive, sacrifices must be made.

Appearance

The Mousemallow is a small rodent piñata with large ears, shaped similarly to the outline of a tennis racket, and a long tail. Most of its body is covered in different shades of pink and purple. It also has small whiskers and dark violet irises. Its ears have jagged designs in the center to its edges. It has a small head, and even a smaller body, and very small feet, and two small front teeth that poke out outside its mouth.

  • 1 Mousemallow in the garden helps to meet the Hootyfruity's Appear requirements. (Pocket Paradise)
  • 2 Mousemallows in the garden helps to meet the Kittyfloss's Appear requirements. (Trouble in Paradise)
  • 2 Mousemallows in the garden helps to meet the Syrupent's Visit requirements. (Trouble in Paradise)
  • 3 Mousemallows in the garden helps meet the Kittyfloss's Visit requirements. (Trouble in Paradise)
  • 5 Mousemallows in the garden helps to meet the Hootyfruity's Visit requirements. (Trouble in Paradise)
  • Eating 1 Mousemallow helps to meet the Kittyfloss's Romance requirements.
  • Eating 1 Mousemallow meets the Fourheads's Trick requirements. (Trouble in Paradise)
  • Eating 1 Mousemallow meets the Lickatoad's Trick requirements.(Trouble in Paradise)
  • Eating 3 Mousemallows helps to meet the Hootyfruity's Resident requirements.(Trouble in Paradise)
  • The Mousemallow scares away Elephanillas, both resident and non resident.
  • Eating 1 Mousemallow meet the Syrupent Resident requirements (Trouble in Paradise)

Housing

The Mousemallow Home is an old wooden clock that the Mousemallow modified to be a house for themselves.

Rumors

  • Leafos says that Elephanillas don’t like Mousemallows for some odd reason. This is true, as having Mousemallows anywhere near Elephanillas will make them run off. They will also prevent non-resident Elephanillas from coming inside the garden.

Glitches and Errors

  • In the original Viva Piñata, all of the Mousemallow's variants appear to be white with only a hint of what their actual color is supposed to be. In Trouble in Paradise, this was fixed, and they appear more vibrant.

In the TV Series

Monty Mousemallow

Monty Mousemallow, also known simply as just Monty, is the main antagonist of the episode "Mouse Flap". He is a mean, crazy, and paranoid Mousemallow who likes to fight piñatas. Monty follows Franklin everywhere, trying his best to get revenge at Franklin. When Monty takes too many accidental blows, he goes to the hospital. Franklin and his friends come see him. Seeing his chance, Monty ties up Fergy and challenges Franklin to a fight. However, Franklin was accidently standing on his oxygen tank, so Monty went unconscious. Franklin decided to end the grudge, and pretended to be defeated. When Monty woke up, he thought he defeated Franklin and was happy. It was also the front page in the Newspaper. He then became friends with Franklin.

Meinhardt Mousemallow

Meinhardt Mousemallow is a Mousemallow who works out at the gym and is considered to be “unbreakable.” Fergy Fudgehog works out at the gym with the aid of Meinhardt so that he may become unbreakable at parties. However, his plan backfires when this turns him into a spectacle that attracts other people to try and destroy him, and even further when he changes into a musclebound brute that goes on a rampage after Langston insults him. In the end, Fergy sits on a couch and loses all his built muscles due to not working out for a minute, ironically declaring working out as unhealthy. Meinhardt also loses his muscles, and complains about having to build it up all over again.

Trivia

  • Leafos gives you a choice between two seeds at the beginning of the game. Planting the purple seed will give you a Turnip, and will help you to achieve the Mousemallow's Appear requirements faster.
  • To obtain twins, the player must finish the maze within 28 seconds (or 8 seconds remaining on clock). (Trouble in Paradise)
